Bynum Landmark

The Montana Dinosaur Center

Right here in Bynum, one of the most significant paleontological discoveries in North America happened — and the Montana Dinosaur Center preserves and shares that story with the world.

The Two Medicine Formation surrounding Bynum has yielded extraordinary fossils, including Maiasaura peeblesorum — the "good mother lizard" — discovered nearby and now Montana's state fossil. The center offers guided tours, hands-on exhibits, and field experiences that draw visitors from across the country to our small town.

June 1 through Labor Day the Center runs hands-on Montana Dinosaur Fossil Expedition digs — a rare chance to work alongside paleontologists in the field.

The Bynum Civic Club is proud to support the Center as a cornerstone of our community's heritage and economy.

Community Gathering Place

Bynum Civic Park

Bynum Civic Park is county-owned land maintained by Civic Club volunteers and open to everyone year-round.

Whether it's a family reunion, a summer picnic, or just an afternoon outside, the park is here for the whole community. The Civic Club organizes regular maintenance days and seasonal improvements to keep it a place residents are proud of.

The park's new structure was funded by the Choteau/Bynum Community Tourism Grant Committee.

Local Shop

T-Rex Agate Shop

Bynum sits in some of the richest agate and fossil country in North America, and the T-Rex Agate Shop is where you can take a piece of it home.

The shop was founded by Dave Trexler — the same Dave Trexler who founded the Montana Dinosaur Center. It carries on a legacy that runs even deeper: Dave's mother, Marion Brandvold, ran the agate shop before him and made one of the most consequential fossil discoveries in American paleontology.

Marion found baby Maiasaura fossils and kept them in a coffee can at the shop. When she showed them to Jack Horner in 1979, it set off the research that changed how scientists understand dinosaur parenting and behavior — and put Bynum on the paleontological map. The shop specializes in Montana agates, fossils, minerals, and gems, many sourced right from the surrounding Two Medicine country.
